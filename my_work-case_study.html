SFI website

Design foundations

Project's objective

Redesigning website for Students' Informatics Festival (SFI)

Limitations

  • 2 month deadline
  • part-time working programers (volunteers)

Persona

Lena Kowalska

  • Age: 22 years
  • Gender: Female
  • Residence: Krakow, Poland
  • Occupation: Student (Management and New Media); Intern
  • Hobbies: Photography, Meeting friends, New technologies
  • Favorite brand: Apple

Lena, a twenty-two-year-old student from Kraków, is a passionate photographer who is not only fascinated by the art of imagery but also follows the latest technological trends. Her enthusiasm for new things combines with her desire to spend time with friends, where she shares her passion and creativity.

Goals & needs

in terms of SFI website

  • I like interactive, colorful elements on websites. Even simple animations captivate me. I believe that a website should be dynamic and engaging for the user.
  • I like good quality graphics.
  • I like to know what I will gain from this festival (certificate).
  • I want the option to change the website's theme (light-dark).
  • I need easy access to speakers and sponsors (prestige of the event).
  • I need easy contact with SFI organizers.
  • A chillout zone and contests would convince me to attend SFI.

Frustrations

in terms of SFI website

  • Some elements of the current website aren't well displayed on mobile.
  • The excess and disorder of information on the website annoy me.
  • In the menu, I cannot find quickly the things that I want.
  • The Auditorium Maximum map is unreadable.
  • In the schedule, it's hard to distinguish what is a lecture, what is a workshop, and what is a Lightning Talk.
  • In the workshops, there is no information that there are limited places or the registration period.
  • Specific workshops and lectures lack information about their exact location.

Design comparison

SFI website before

Home page

Poor responsiveness

The menu is in a form of a hamburger despite the fact that it is a desktop view.

Repetitive data

the name of the Festival is mentioned 3 times within one desktop view.

Bad visual hierarchy

The orange graphic draws most attention, therefore the most valid information - the place and date of the Event - is hard to notice.

Confusing Information Architecture

The section with lectures schedule has no header. The filters are mixed despite the fact that they represent different catergories.

Lecture

Useless information

The orange graphic with the name of the event draws most attention yet it is irrelevant for this lecture subpage. The user must have already seen it on the home page.

Bad Information Architecture

First information under the title is again name and number of the edition, which is repeatedly mentioned. The name of the lecturer should be integral with the title of the lecture, yet it is placed far from it. The date is not distinguished in any way from other, less important information.
